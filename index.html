Dubai is one of the fastest-changing cities in the world. Dubai is a large city in the country of the United Arab Emirates. It sits on the Persian Gulf, right next to the ocean. Dubai is a port city. But it has a completely desert climate. In the past, Dubai was not a big city. Most of its citizens lived simple lives. They earned money by catching fish, or diving for valuable ocean pearls. Until recent years, most people in the area around Dubai lived the same way they had lived for hundreds of years before.

Thailand has a tropical monsoon climate with plenty of suns, high rainfall and high humidity. This climate leads to the fact that Thailand is rich in a huge variety of flora and fauna. There are lots of forests, mountains, rivers, valleys and gorges in Thailand that are great places to visit. Thailand is famous for its beautiful white sand beaches and clear blue seas.

Sri Lanka is an island off the southern tip of Hindustan, large and green, surrounded by ocean, famous for its historical and natural attractions. Fruits, seafood, tea, surfing, mountains, animals in a slightly wild contrast from the metropolis to the countryside - this is a great place for lovers of active recreation without unnecessary luxury.

Almost all of Cuba is one continuous coastline with beach resorts. Closer to the center of the island are more authentic Cuban towns and villages with local flavor and historical attractions. Cuba is a fairly compact country, and you can easily travel around it all in a couple of weeks.

Egypt is generously gifted with nature and history. This country has one of the seven wonders of the world - the great pyramids, it owns the mysterious Sphinx, on its territory there are many temples left by one of the greatest civilizations of antiquity. Egypt has the great Nile, and a piece of the Sahara, and oases lost in the desert, and ancient Alexandria, and biblical shrines on the Sinai Peninsula, and orientally colorful Cairo. And most importantly, the countless sights of the country of the pharaohs can be explored, combining with a beach holiday in the popular "all inclusive" format.

A city built on water, a city where there are probably more canals than roads, and boats successfully replace cars, Venice has a truly indescribable atmosphere. Narrow streets, bright textured faded old walls, gondoliers' songs, trattorias on small squares, shops with phantasmagoric masks and masquerade costumes, wine bars with tables on the banks of the canals seem to enchant everyone who comes to this city.

The climate of Italy is typically Mediterranean, with warm and mild winters and hot and dry summers. The Alps protect the country from cold northern winds, and sea currents provide warmth and moisture. However, despite the geographical features of the location, there is little precipitation.

The history of Burgundy is connected with the toponym of the same name, which appeared on the map of Europe during the Great Migration of Peoples. The word itself goes back to the designation of the Germanic tribe of the Burgundians, who, led by Gundahar, created their kingdom in 413 with the capital in Worms (Upper Rhine). After the invasion of the Huns, the settlement area of ​​the Burgundians shifted to the Geneva region, and then Burgundy expanded in the southwest direction along the Rhone River (southeast France).

Romania is one of the largest countries in Eastern Europe. And although the climate here is characterized as moderately continental, weather conditions in different areas of the country can vary greatly depending on the proximity to the Black Sea or the mountains.

Iceland is a young country, and the process of environmental development is not yet complete. Travelers and tourists flock to Iceland not for duty-free shopping or cheap daisies, but to see the main elements of nature - earth, water, fire and air - in a way that you won't find anywhere else on the planet. Iceland has 180 volcanoes, 18 of which are active. Although Icelandic volcanoes can be very active, most are located far from settlements.
